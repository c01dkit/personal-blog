First flight

The epidemic came to an end and InforSec had its first offline meeting in Shenzhen. Brother Fu Chong was invited to share as a speaker, and we discussed our intention to form a group for the Shenzhen trip and attend the meeting together. This is my first time to fly! It was also my first time to participate in a large Shinan offline meeting. I was quite nervous before coming, and arranged for my roommate to help me format my computer if I didn't come back safely (even joked about writing a good suicide note before leavinghh), but it actually felt like a bus (because there would be bumps up and down, and the high speed train only swayed left and right). Two hours of flight, a little bit more than enough. The plane is also relatively quiet, because there is no network at high altitude, so there is no phone calls, video playback or anything. The scenery above the clouds is quite beautiful, always sunny, deep blue sky, endless sea of clouds, quiet and mysterious. It would be nice to have the ability to fly. I would love to travel between the clouds!

Some feelings

The focus of the meeting is actually not to listen to the report, but to meet the big brother, social (offline communication, add friends), the first day and other people together to add Mr. Huang's WeChat, the second day took the initiative to add Mr. Zhou's WeChat (but have not asked questions), and so we have a few days after the business trip, back to Hangzhou, and then have the opportunity to ask for advice!

In terms of gain, it is actually a change in mindset and a fuller understanding. In the roundtable, the teachers talked about the traits they want in a PhD student, from primary to secondary, which are strong self-drive, curiosity about technology, and resilience. If you have self-drive, you will communicate and solve problems in time, instead of passively waiting for the group meeting when you are asked to say that there is no progress; if you have curiosity about technology, you will keep searching for microscopic exploration and get self-improvement; if you have tenacity, you will not be deterred by failure and rejection again and again, and finally fix the right result. As for cleverness and diligence, that is the last thing I would consider "cleverness". Reflecting on the fact that I started to do the project on my own, I have been rather passive and passive, searching for information without a clear goal orientation, trying methods without thinking deeply, and not even thinking clearly about the scenario, whether it is necessary to do, what the problems and challenges are. How can we make some progress if we deny ourselves from the very beginning? What should we do in the future? It is better to have a proper attitude towards research and not to do research for the sake of research. In addition, you should know some common sense, such as journals, conferences, related teams, latest progress and so on, and not to work behind closed doors.
